Frequently Asked Questions

How does the term system work (Prelim, Midterm, Finals)?

Darkboard uses a three-term grading system to track your academic progress throughout the semester:

  • Prelim (Weeks 1-5) - The first evaluation period covering the initial weeks of the semester. This sets the baseline for your initial grades.
  • Midterm (Weeks 6-10) - The halfway point of the semester, covering content from the first half. This is a major assessment that counts significantly toward your final grade.
  • Finals (Weeks 11-16) - The end-of-semester examination covering all material taught throughout the term. This is typically the most weighted assessment and occurs during the last weeks of the semester.

Your overall grade is calculated by combining scores from all three terms. The average is displayed on the Grades page for each subject, helping you track your progress throughout the semester.

How do I view my grades?

To view your grades, navigate to the Grades page from the main menu. Your grades are displayed in a comprehensive table showing your midterm, final, and average grades for each subject.

How can I check my subject schedule?

Visit the Schedule page to view your weekly subject schedule. You can see the time, subject, and room for each class.

How do I add a new subject?

On the Subjects page, click the "+" button to add a new subject. Fill in the subject name, teacher, time, and description, then click "Save Subject".

How do I track my assignments?

On the Subjects page, select a subject and navigate to the Assignments tab. You can view, add, edit, and delete assignments for each subject.

How do I change my profile information?

Go to the Profile page and click "Edit Profile" to update your personal information. Changes are saved automatically.
